Dear Frosh,

Good luck and take care. You're about to start an adventure!

David Boroto, EngSci 1T8 + PEY

Dear Frosh of 2T2,

You are about to embark on a wild and transformative adventure. The years ahead of you will be unlike anything you have ever experienced in your life so far. You will be pushed and challenged, but you will also be rewarded with so many unforgettable memories. And you will meet amazing people who will go on to become your best friends. It's cliche, but it's true!

There is a place for everyone at UofT Engineering (a.k.a. SKULE). No matter what your interests are, SKULE is full of whacky and quirky individuals - you are bound to find your people who share your passions and interests. Embrace the journey, open yourself up to new experiences and explore your interests. You will learn amazing things from your professors inside the classroom, but if you're doing things right you will learn even more from your peers outside the classroom.

Don't worry too much about your grades - they're important, but they don't define you. Spend a little less time on that problem set and a little more time in that design team, or that club that interests you. Take care of yourself. Take time to learn about yourself, your mental health and what you need to be at your best. What worked for you in high school may not work for you in university. And that's okay! You will go through some tough times, but that is a part of the process. Embrace it, learn from it, and grow. You will become a stronger person for it. Find the people who will support you through your journey and be your rock when you need it. There is always someone there to talk to.

Say yes to everything! Especially in the first week. If ever in doubt, say yes! The picture I've attached* is from my first day on campus during F!ROSH week, immediately after getting dyed purple... Embrace discomfort and experience the wild and whacky world of SKULE. It is indeed very... strange (but in a good way!)

I don't know who you are, but I am so very excited for you as you embark on this journey. I hope to hear about how SKULE transforms your life, and about all the experiences and memories you will create. You are joining a wonderful community. If ever you need anything - advice, support, or a funny story to make you laugh - always feel free to reach out.

S.K.U.L.E. - Engineering UofT! We are, we are, we are, we are, we are the engineers! ;)

Yours sincerely,

David Boroto
EngSci 1T8 + PEY
