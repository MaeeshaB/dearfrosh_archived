Dear Frosh,

Challenge Yourself to Have Fun

Kaija, EngSci 2T5

Dear Future Engineer,

Before I came to UofT, I was really nervous. I was moving away from home, I wasn’t sure if I could live on my own, cook for myself, and keep my place clean. I was also terrified that I wouldn’t be able to do engineering, that I would fail my classes, and that I wouldn’t make any friends.

During my first year I was surprised by how much fun I had. I met so many interesting people during frosh week and in my classes. I joined a club even though I didn’t really do extracurriculars in highschool. In my second semester, I started going to more community and club events.

I wish I’d realized sooner how fun being in engineering could be. That school is achievable while still being able to enjoy yourself. There are so many resources and ways to manage your schoolwork, but the most important thing is that you don’t have to do it alone.

I want you to know that there is always support available to you. Whether it’s from your profs and TAs, your friends and family, or the engineering community. It’s easier to do something challenging when you’re not doing it alone. That’s what I think is the most important part of UofT engineering.

So challenge yourself. Join a club, talk to a new person, ask questions because that’s what Skule is all about.

Also please feel free to reach out if you have any questions or just want to talk to someone!

Warmly,

Kaija
EngSci 2T5
