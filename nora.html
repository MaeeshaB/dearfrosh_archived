Dear Frosh,

Guess What, You DIDN’T Peak in High School

Nora, ChemE 2T1

Dear First Year Nora,

Just wait until you find out that you’re going to be graduating with two rings on those fingers… ;)

You’ll be shocked to learn that you married an ECE, and your best friends would fly out to see you get married, from as far away as Finland. It turned out that getting a 4.0 didn’t actually matter, not that you were anywhere close to it…, because you still landed your dream job (ironically in oil&gas, not environmental like you originally planned). You’re going to change a lot, and then some. Now this is about the point where I know you start to skim, so let me put my advice to you in a way “we'' understand

1. Happiness is a Choice. There’s no greater achievement that radiates like a positive mindset, and that’s the only type of trophy that won't lose its shine. Start on that now.

2. Go to your EngSci Friend’s Birthday Party, Even When You’re Exhausted from Classes. The best thing that’ll ever happen to you is waiting there with four slices of pizza on his plate and is NOT going to share. He will, however, offer to split a piece of cake with you and you’ll be grateful for the rest of your life that you went out that night

3. This Isn’t a Game of Among Us, You Are Not an Imposter... oh wait, you might not even know what that is. Your future best friends are patiently waiting for you to approach them with your problem set. Neither of you will figure it out, but at least you made some friends for life. You’ll also laugh about it four years later when you realize you still don’t know the answers

4. Engineering is a TEAM EFFORT, as is Life. I cannot emphasize this enough. From past exams being posted, to working together on homework, no one ever did it by themselves. There’s no extra ribbon at the end for those who made it on their own. The sooner you realize this, the less you will struggle

5. First and Foremost, You Are a Daughter, You Are a Sister, You Are a Friend and You Are Loved. You will be super grateful for your degree, the clubs, and any experiences that open up opportunities, but nothing will compare to the relationships you nurture. Your loved ones will support you through the good and the bad, so make sure they know just how much they are loved and appreciated. It will go a long way

To wrap it up, be open to all the changes and experiences to come, but don’t forget what truly matters to you. Your four years are going to be amazing because that’s what you made of it. I still can’t believe after four years you still couldn’t figure out how to cook... but I won’t hold that against you.

Cheers,

Nora
ChemE 2T1
